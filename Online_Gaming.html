Online Games History

Online gaming has evolved significantly since the late 1970s and early 1980s, when simple text-based games like "MUD" (Multi-User Dungeon) introduced multiplayer interaction. The 1990s saw the rise of graphical MMORPGs like "Meridian 59" and "Ultima Online," paving the way for massive hits like "World of Warcraft" in the early 2000s. The emergence of broadband internet in the late 1990s and early 2000s further transformed the landscape, allowing for more immersive and expansive online worlds. In recent years, the rise of mobile gaming, esports, and cloud gaming has continued to shape the industry, making online gaming more accessible and diverse than ever.
